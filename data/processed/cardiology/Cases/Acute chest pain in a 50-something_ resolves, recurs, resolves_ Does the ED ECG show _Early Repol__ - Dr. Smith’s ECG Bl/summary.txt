Acute chest pain in a 50-something: resolves, recurs, resolves — Does the ED ECG show “Early Repol”?
KEYWORDS: ecg, s-wave, ecgs, minutes, ecg time, early repolarization, early, lad, repolarization, time, pain, minutes ecg, terminal qrs, terminal, occlusion

Case A man in his 50s called 911 for acute chest pain. He had a significant history of myocardial infarction and coronary artery disease with multiple stents (D1 and RCA), and ischemic cardiomyopathy. Medics recorded serial ECGs. ECG 1 (time zero): Diagnostic of LAD occlusion (LAD OMI). The pain began to improve, and serial ECGs were recorded. ECG 2 (time 2 minutes) ECG 3 (time 16 minutes) ECG 4 (time 32 minutes): The ECG normalized.
