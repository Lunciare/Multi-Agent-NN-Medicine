Acute chest pain, 2 years in a row
KEYWORDS: pericarditis, pericardial, normal, omi, acute, visit, elevated, pericardial effusion, cardiac, effusion, laboratory, enhancement consistent, crp, crp elevated, depression

A man in his 30s presented twice, one year apart, with acute substernal chest pain. Visit 1 History: Acute substernal chest pain, shortness of breath, diaphoresis within 3 hours of onset. Obesity, no other cardiac risk factors. ECG findings: Multiple leads with ST elevation meeting STEMI criteria, but with non-hyperacute T waves, benign ST morphology, Spodick sign, and PR depression. ST elevation in lead II greater than in III, and no reciprocal ST depression in aVL. Emergent angiogram: Normal coronary arteries.
