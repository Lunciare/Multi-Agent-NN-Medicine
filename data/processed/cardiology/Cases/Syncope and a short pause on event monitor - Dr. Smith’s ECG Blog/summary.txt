A middle-aged man experienced sudden syncope after a brief episode of light-headedness while standing at home. He awoke on the ground, uncertain of the duration of loss of consciousness. Initial inpatient evaluation and telemetry were unremarkable, and he was discharged with a 4-week event monitor and EP follow-up. Even a single episode of cardiac syncope, if it occurs at the wrong moment (e.g., while driving), can be fatal.
