Thrombolytics were administered. The second troponin was lower at 12 ng/mL. The ECG showed high acuteness. Although there was subacute infarction with falling troponin, recurrent chest pain and hyperacute T-waves implied re-occlusion. Follow-Up Cardiology did not take the patient immediately to the cath lab due to concern that the patient would not tolerate lying flat. Echocardiography showed a dilated right ventricle with good left ventricular function, prompting concern for pulmonary embolism. Pain resolved and troponins trended down. The right ventricle was dilated due to right ventricular myocardial infarction, not pulmonary embolism. Next-day angiography showed a 90% proximal right coronary artery culprit lesion that was stented. The right coronary artery was reperfused, almost certainly due to thrombolytics.
