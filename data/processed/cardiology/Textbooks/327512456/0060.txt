Healthcare providers, which are facing increasing pressure to implement patient- and family-centred care into their practice, have to engage in multidisciplinary aspects of care, from building relationships with patients and families, to engaging in organizational leadership strategies. But they are also constantly challenged by the provision of care under conditions of constrained resources, especially in this modern era where the epidemiological transition has determined a tremendous expansion of individuals with chronic illnesses and an increase in costs. Patient- and family-centred care in cardiovascular conditions Cardiovascular diseases are recognized as a major health problem worldwide. According to the literature, they represent the leading cause of death (see Chapter 1). For example, in the US, they account for 17% of all health expenditure.69 However, the burden of cardiovascular diseases comes not only from mortality but also from the consequences of living with the disease.
