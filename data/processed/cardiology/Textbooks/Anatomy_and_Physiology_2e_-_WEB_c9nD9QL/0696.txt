The ilium forms the large, fan-shaped superior portion, the ischium forms the posteroinferior portion, and the pubis forms the anteromedial portion. Theiliumis the fan-like, superior region that forms the largest part of the hip bone. It is firmly united to the sacrum at the largely immobilesacroiliac joint(seeFigure 8.12). Theischiumforms the posteroinferior region of each hip bone. It supports the body when sitting. Thepubisforms the anterior portion of the hip bone. The pubis curves medially, where it joins to the pubis of the opposite hip bone at a specialized joint called thepubic symphysis.
