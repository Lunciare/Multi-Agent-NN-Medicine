On the anterior maxilla, just below the orbit, is the infraorbital foramen. This is the point of exit for a sensory nerve that supplies the nose, upper lip, and anterior cheek. On the inferior skull, thepalatine processfrom each maxillary bone can be seen joining together at the midline to form the anterior three-quarters of the hard palate (seeFigure 7.8a). Thehard palateis the bony plate that forms the roof of the mouth and floor of the nasal cavity, separating the oral and nasal cavities.
