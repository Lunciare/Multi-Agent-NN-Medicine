The thoracolumbar output, through the various sympathetic ganglia, reaches all of these organs. The cranial component of the parasympathetic system projects from the eye to part of the intestines. Short and Long Reflexes Somatic reflexes involve sensory neurons that connect sensory receptors to the CNS and motor neurons that project back out to the skeletal muscles. Visceral reflexes that involve the thoracolumbar or craniosacral systems share similar connections. However, there are reflexes that do not need to involve any CNS components. Along reflexhas afferent branches that enter the spinal cord or brain and involve the efferent branches, as previously explained. A short reflexis completely peripheral and only involves the local integration of sensory input with motor output (Figure 15.8).
