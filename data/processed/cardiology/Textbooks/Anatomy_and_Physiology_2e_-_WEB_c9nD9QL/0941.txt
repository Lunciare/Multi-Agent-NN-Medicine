After this occurs, ATP is converted to ADP and P by the intrinsicATPaseactivity of myosin. The energy released during ATP hydrolysis changes the angle i of the myosin head into a cocked position (Figure 10.11e). The myosin head is now in position for further movement. When the myosin head is cocked, myosin is in a high-energy configuration. This energy is expended as the myosin head moves through the power stroke, and at the end of the power stroke, the myosin head is in a low-energy position. After the power stroke, ADP is released; however, the formed cross-bridge is still in place, and actin and myosin are bound together. As long as ATP is available, it readily attaches to myosin, the cross-bridge cycle can recur, and muscle contraction can continue. Note
