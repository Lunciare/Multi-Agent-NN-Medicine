The other processes of the neuron are dendrites, which receive information from other neurons at specialized areas of contact calledsynapses. The dendrites are usually highly branched processes, providing locations for other neurons to communicate with the cell body. Information flows through a neuron from the dendrites, across the cell body, and down the axon. Where the axon emerges from the cell body, there is a special region referred to as theaxon hillock. This is a tapering of the cell body toward the axon fiber. Within the axon hillock, the cytoplasm changes to a solution of limited components calledaxoplasm. Because the axon hillock represents the beginning of the axon, it is also referred to as theinitial segment.
