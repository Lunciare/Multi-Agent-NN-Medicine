Plasma Cells Another type of lymphocyte of importance is the plasma cell. Aplasma cellis a B cell that has differentiated in response to antigen binding, and has thereby gained the ability to secrete soluble antibodies. These cells differ in morphology from standard B and T cells in that they contain a large amount of cytoplasm packed with the protein- synthesizing machinery known as rough endoplasmic reticulum.
