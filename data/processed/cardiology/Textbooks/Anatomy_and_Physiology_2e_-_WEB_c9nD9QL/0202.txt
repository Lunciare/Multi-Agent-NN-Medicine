Whenever a substance exists in greater concentration on one side of a semipermeable membrane, such as the cell membranes, any substance that can move down its concentration gradient across the membrane will do so. Consider substances that can easily diffuse through the lipid bilayer of the cell membrane, such as the gases oxygen (O ) and CO . O generally diffuses into cells because it is more concentrated outside of them, and CO typically 2 2 2 2 diffuses out of cells because it is more concentrated inside of them.
