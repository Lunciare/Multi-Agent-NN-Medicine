The region of the pulp cavity that runs through the root of the tooth is called the root canal. Surrounding the pulp cavity isdentin, a bone-like tissue. In the root of each tooth, the dentin is covered by an even harder bone-like layer calledcementum. In the crown of each tooth, the dentin is covered by an outer layer ofenamel, the hardest substance in the body (Figure 23.11). Although enamel protects the underlying dentin and pulp cavity, it is still nonetheless susceptible to mechanical and chemical erosion, or what is known as tooth decay. The most common form, dental caries (cavities) develops when colonies of bacteria feeding on sugars in the mouth release acids that cause soft tissue inflammation and degradation of the calcium crystals of the enamel.
