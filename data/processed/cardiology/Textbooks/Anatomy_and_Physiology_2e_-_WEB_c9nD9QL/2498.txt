The right and left pleurae, which enclose the right and left lungs, respectively, are separated by the mediastinum. The pleurae consist of two layers. Thevisceral pleurais the layer that is superficial to the lungs, and extends into and lines the lung fissures (Figure 22.14). In contrast, theparietal pleura is the outer layer that connects to the thoracic wall, the mediastinum, and the diaphragm. The visceral and parietal pleurae connect to each other at the hilum. Thepleural cavityis the space between the visceral and parietal layers.
