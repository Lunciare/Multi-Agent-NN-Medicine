The standard isotope of carbon is12C, commonly called carbon twelve.12C has six protons and six neutrons, for a mass number of twelve. All of the isotopes of carbon have the same number of protons; therefore,13C has seven neutrons, and14C has eight neutrons. The different isotopes of an element can also be indicated with the mass number hyphenated (for example, C-12 instead of12C).
