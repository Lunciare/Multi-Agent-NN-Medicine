HPV strains are classified as high or low risk according to their potential to cause cancer. Though most HPV infections do not cause disease, the disruption of normal cellular functions in the low-risk forms of HPV can cause the human host to develop genital warts. Often, the body is able to clear an HPV infection by normal immune responses within 2 years.
