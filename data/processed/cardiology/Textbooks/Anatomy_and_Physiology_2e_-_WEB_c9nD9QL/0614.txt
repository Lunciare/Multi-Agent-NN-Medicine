Access for free at openstax.org 7.4 • The Thoracic Cage 259 7.4 The Thoracic Cage LEARNING OBJECTIVES By the end of this section, you will be able to: • Discuss the components that make up the thoracic cage • Identify the parts of the sternum and define the sternal angle • Discuss the parts of a rib and rib classifications The thoracic cage (rib cage) forms the thorax (chest) portion of the body. It consists of the 12 pairs of ribs with their costal cartilages and the sternum (Figure 7.32). The ribs are anchored posteriorly to the 12 thoracic vertebrae (T1–T12). The thoracic cage protects the heart and lungs. FIGURE7.32 Thoracic Cage The thoracic cage is formed by the (a) sternum and (b) 12 pairs of ribs with their costal cartilages. The ribs are anchored posteriorly to the 12 thoracic vertebrae. The sternum consists of the manubrium, body, and xiphoid process. The ribs are classified as true ribs (1–7) and false ribs (8–12).
