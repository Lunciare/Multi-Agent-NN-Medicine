HOMEOSTATIC IMBALANCES The Parotid Glands: Mumps Infections of the nasal passages and pharynx can attack any salivary gland. The parotid glands are the usual site of infection with the virus that causes mumps (paramyxovirus). Mumps manifests by enlargement and inflammation of the parotid glands, causing a characteristic swelling between the ears and the jaw. Symptoms include fever and throat pain, which can be severe when swallowing acidic substances such as orange juice. In about one-third of males who are past puberty, mumps also causes testicular inflammation, typically affecting only one testis and rarely resulting in sterility.
