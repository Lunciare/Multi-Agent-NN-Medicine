Two types ofpancreatic nucleaseare responsible for their digestion:deoxyribonuclease, which digests DNA, andribonuclease, which digests RNA. The nucleotides produced by this digestion are further broken down by two intestinal brush border enzymes (nucleosidaseandphosphatase) into pentoses, phosphates, and nitrogenous bases, which can be absorbed through the alimentary canal wall.
