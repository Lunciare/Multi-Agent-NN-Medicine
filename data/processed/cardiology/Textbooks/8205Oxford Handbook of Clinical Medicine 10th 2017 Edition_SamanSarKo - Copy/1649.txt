Acute Pancreatitis (BISAP). Fig 13.45 Axial CT of the abdomen (with IV and PO contrast media) show- ing a pancreatic pseudocyst occupying the lesser sac of the abdomen pos- terior to the stomach. It is called a ‘pseudocyst’ because it is not a true cyst, rather a collection of fl uid in the lesser sac (ie not lined by epi/endothelium). It develops at ≥6wks. The cyst fl uid is of low attenuation compared with the stomach contents because it has not been enhanced by the contrast media.
