Holiday heart syndrome Binge drinking in a person without any clinical evidence of heart disease may result in acute cardiac rhythm and/or conduction disturbances, which is called holiday heart syndrome (note that recreational use of marijuana may have similar eff ects). The most common rhythm disorders are supraventricular tachyarrhyth- mia and AF (consider this diagnosis in patients without structural heart disease who present with new-onset AF). The prognosis is excellent, especially in young patients without structural heart disease.
