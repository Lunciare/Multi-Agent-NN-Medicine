Insomnia This is trivial—until we ourselves have a few sleepless nights. Then sleep becomes the most desirable thing imaginable, and bestowing it the best thing we can do, like relieving pain. But don’t give drugs without looking for a cause. • Self-limiting: Jet lag; stress; shift work; in hospital. We need less sleep as we age. • Psychic: Depression; anxiety; mania; grief; psychomotor agitation/psychosis. • Organic: Drugs (many; eg caff eine; mefl oquine; nicotine withdrawal); nocturia; alcohol; pain (eg acid refl ux—worse on lying down); itch; tinnitus; asthma; dys- tonias; obstructive sleep apnoea (p194); dementia; restless leg syndrome (p698, check ferritin). Rarer: encephalitis (eg West Nile virus) and encephalopathy (Whipple’s; pellagra; HIV; prion diseases, eg CJD, p696, and fatal familial insomnia). :Sleep hygiene. No daytime naps; don’t turn in till you feel sleepy; regular bed- time routines. Keep a room for sleep; don’t eat or work in it (not viable for much of the world).
