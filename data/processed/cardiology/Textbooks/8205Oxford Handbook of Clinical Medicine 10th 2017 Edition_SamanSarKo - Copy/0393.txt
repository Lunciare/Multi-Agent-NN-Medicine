Acquired causes: rheumatic fever, carcinoid syndrome. Symptoms: Dyspnoea; fatigue; oedema; ascites. Signs: Dys- morphic facies (congenital causes); prominent a wave in JVP; RV heave. In mild stenosis, there is an ejection click, ejection systolic murmur (which radiates to the left shoulder); widely split S2. In severe stenosis, the murmur becomes longer and obscures A2. Cardiac catheterization is diagnostic. Treatment: Pulmonary valvuloplasty or valvotomy. Pulmonary regurgitation Causes: Any cause of pulmonary hypertension (p194).
