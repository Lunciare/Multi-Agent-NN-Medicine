Convulsions are the motor signs of elect rical discharges. Elements of a seizure Some patients may experience a preceding prodrome last- ing hours or days in which there may be a change in mood or behaviour. An aura implies a focal seizure, often, but not necessarily, from the temporal lobe. It may be a strange feeling in the gut, an experience such as déjà vu or strange smells or fl ashing lights. Post-ictally there may be headache, confusion, and myalgia; or temporary weakness after a focal seizure in the motor cortex (Todd’s palsy, p712), or dysphasia following a focal seizure in the temporal lobe.
