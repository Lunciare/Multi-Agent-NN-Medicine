Investigations are all normal, and medications do not seem to work. It is tempting to dismiss such patients as malingerers, but often this conclusion comes from the clinician approaching the problem from the wrong angle. The patient has symptoms that are real and disa- bling to them, and that will not improve without help. Perhaps a more pragmatic approach is to take advice from the Danish philosopher Kierkegaard who wrote to a friend in 1835, ‘What I really lack is to be clear in my mind what I am to do, not what I am to know ... The thing is to understand myself ... to fi nd a truth which is true for me.’ Listen to the patient and accept their story. Then help them to focus on what they can do to improve their situation, and to move away from dwelling on fi nding a physical answer to their symptoms.
