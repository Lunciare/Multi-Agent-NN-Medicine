Invasive disease occurs when the shed eggs are ingested via faeco–oral transmission. Neurocysticercosis is due to larval cysts infecting the CNS. Presentation: Determined by site and number of lesions (cysticerci) within the brain/spinal cord. Epilepsy in 70%. Focal neurology in ~20%: motor/sensory loss, lan- guage disturbance, invol- untary movements. Also headache, visual loss, meningitis, hydrocepha- lus, cognitive impairment. Diagnosis: CT/MRI imaging plus serology. Treatment: Seizure control (evidence on drug choice, length of treatment or prophylaxis).
