Passing the tube Nurses are experts and will ask you (who may never have passed one) to do so only when they fail—so the fi rst question to ask is: ‘Have you asked the charge-nurse from the ward next door?’ • Wear non-sterile gloves and an apron to protect both you and the patient. • Explain the procedure. Take a new, cool (hence less fl exible) tube. Have a cup of water to hand. Lubricate well with aqueous gel. • Use the tube, by holding it against the patient’s head, to estimate the length required to get from the nostril to the back of the throat. • Place lubricated tube in nostril with its natural curve promoting passage down, rather than up. The right nostril is often easier than the left but, if feasible, ask the patient for their preference.
