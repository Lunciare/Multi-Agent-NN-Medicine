Dyspepsia Dyspepsia and indigestion (p252) are broad terms. Dyspepsia is defi ned as one or more of post-prandial fullness, early satiety (unable to fi nish meal), and/or epigastric or retrosternal pain or burning. ‘Indigestion’ reported by the patient can refer to dyspepsia, bloating, nausea, and vomiting. Try to fi nd out exactly what your pa- tient means and when these symptoms occur in relation to meals, eg the classic symptoms of peptic ulcers occur 2–5 hours after a meal and on an empty stomach. Look for alarm symptoms (see p248); these have high negative predictive value. If all patients with dyspepsia undergo endoscopy, <33% have clinically signifi cant fi ndings.11 Myocardial infaction may present as ‘indigestion’.
