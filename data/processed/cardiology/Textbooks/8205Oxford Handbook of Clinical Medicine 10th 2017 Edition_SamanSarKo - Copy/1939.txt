Weaning When planning removal of an NGT in situ for decompression or relief of obstruction, it is wise to wean it so that the patient manages well without it. Drainage should be <750mL/24 hours for successful weaning. • First it should be on free drainage with, eg, 4hrly aspirations. • Then spigot with 4hrly aspirations. • Then spigot only.
