Management • Treat underlying cause—eg COPD and pulmonary infections. • Treat respiratory failure—in the acute situation give 24% oxygen if PaO2 <8kPa. Monitor ABG and gradually increase oxygen concentration if PaCO2 is stable (p188). In COPD patients, long-term oxygen therapy (LTOT) for 16h/d increases survival (p184). Patients with chronic hypoxia when clinically stable should be assessed for LTOT. • Treat cardiac failure with diuretics such as furosemide, eg 40–160mg/24h PO. Moni- tor U&E and give amiloride or potassium supplements if necessary. Alternative: spironolactone. • Consider venesection if haematocrit >55%. • Consider heart–lung transplantation in young patients.
