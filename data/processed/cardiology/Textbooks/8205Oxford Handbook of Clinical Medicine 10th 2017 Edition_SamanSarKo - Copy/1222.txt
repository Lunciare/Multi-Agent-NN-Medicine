St Vitus’s dance Throughout the middle ages, Europe was plagued by epidemics of ‘dancing mania’, in which affl icted individuals were described to have danced wildly, displaying strange contortions and convulsions until they collapsed from exhaustion. If the affl icted touched a relic of St Vitus they were miraculously cured: observing this, Paracelsus, 16th-century Swiss-German physician and philosopher, described the phenomenon of chorea Sancti Viti (‘St Vitus’s dance’).
