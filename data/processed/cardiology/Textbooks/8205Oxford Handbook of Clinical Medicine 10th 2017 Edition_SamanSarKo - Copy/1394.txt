Seek early advice from palliative care if agitation is escalating or a signifi cant problem. Manage excessive secretions. Noise is generated by turbulent air fl ow and pooling of saliva in the hypopharynx. This may be more distressing for relatives and staff than the patient. There is little evidence that pharmacological agents are benefi cial, though they are commonly used. Repositioning and intermittent suctioning may help.
