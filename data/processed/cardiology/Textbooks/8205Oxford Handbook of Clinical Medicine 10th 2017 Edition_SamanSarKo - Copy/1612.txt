Presentation: Varies according to location and may include obstructive jaundice, pruritus, abdominal pain, weight loss, and anorexia. Investigations: US, CT, and ERCP. MRI has a role for determining extent of invasion in bile duct cancers. Treatment: • Bile duct cancer: surgical resection is the only potentially curative treatment yet ~80% present with inoperable disease. Palliation includes biliary stenting and chemotherapy. • Gallbladder cancer: again, radical surgery is the only chance of cure. Patients with a calcified (‘porcelain’) gallbladder have an increased risk of cancer—pro- phylactic surgery should be considered.
