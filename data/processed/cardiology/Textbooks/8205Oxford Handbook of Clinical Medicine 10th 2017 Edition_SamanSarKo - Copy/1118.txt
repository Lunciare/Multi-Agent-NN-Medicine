Traveller’s diarrhoea Diarrhoea aff ects 20–60% of travellers.20,21 High-risk areas: South Asia, Central and South America, Africa. Major cause = enterotoxigenic E. coli. Prevention: Boil water, cook thoroughly, peel fruit and vegetables. Avoid ice, sal- ads, shellfi sh. Drink with a straw. Hand washing with soap may  risk. Presentation: Most diarrhoea is during fi rst week of travel. Symptoms are often unreliable indicators of aetiology but the following may be indicative: • Enterotoxigenic E. coli: watery diarrhoea preceded by cramps and nausea. • Giardia lamblia: upper GI symptoms, eg bloating, belching. • Campylobacter jejuni and Shigella: colitic symptoms, urgency, cramps. Duration of diarrhoea: most <1wk, 10%>1wk, 5% >2wks, 1%>30d. Treatment: • Oral rehydration.
