Associated features Eye pain ± reduced vision: Think acute glaucoma. Typically elderly, long-sighted people. Constant pain develops rapidly around one eye, radiat- ing to the forehead with markedly reduced vision, visual haloes, and a red, congested eye (p561). Seek expert help at once. If delay in treatment of >1h is likely, give eye drops (eg 0.5% timolol maleate ± 2% pilocarpine) and acetazolamide 500mg PO. Jaw claudication tender with thickened, pulseless temporal arteries: Giant cell arteritis: (p556) Subacute-onset headache with ESR >40mm/h. Exclude in all >50yrs old with a headache that has lasted a few weeks: prompt diagnosis and steroids avoid blindness. Precipitating causes Head trauma: Commonly causes localized pain but can be more generalized. It lasts ~2wks; often resistant to analgesia.
