Inspect (and smell) for signs of chronic liver disease: • Hepatic fetor on breath (p274). • Gynaecomastia. • Clubbing (rare). • Purpura (purple-stained skin, p344). • Scratch marks. • Muscle wasting. • Spider naevi (fi g 2.27, p60). • Palmar erythema. • Jaundice. • Asterixis. Look for signs of malignancy (cachexia, masses), anaemia, jaundice, Virchow’s node. From the end of the bed inspect the abdomen for: • Visible pulsation (aneurysm, p654). • Peristalsis. • Scars. • Masses. • Striae (stretch marks, eg pregnancy). • Distension. • Genitalia. • Herniae. If abdominal wall veins look dilated, assess direction of fl ow. In inferior vena caval (IVC) obstruction, below the umbilicus blood fl ows up; in portal hypertension (caput medusae), fl ow radiates out from the umbilicus. The cough test: While looking at the face, ask the patient to cough. If this causes abdominal pain, fl inching, or a protective movement of hands towards the abdomen, suspect peritonitis.
