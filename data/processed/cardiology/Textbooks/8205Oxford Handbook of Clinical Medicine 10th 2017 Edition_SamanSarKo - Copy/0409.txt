Clinical features: Central chest pain worse on inspiration or lying fl at ± relief by sitting forward. A pericardial friction rub (p46) may be heard. Look for evidence of a pericardial eff usion or cardiac tamponade (see later in topic). Fever may occur. Tests: ECG classically shows concave (saddle-shaped) ST segment elevation and PR depression, but may be normal or non-specifi c (10%); see fi g 3.51.
