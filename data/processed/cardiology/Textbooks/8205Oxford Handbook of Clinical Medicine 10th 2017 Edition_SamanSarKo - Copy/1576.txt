While the strength of his diagnostic convictions combined with his speed and skill at operating is to be admired (the operation took 25 minutes), there is an even more laudable part played in this story. The patient, Mrs Jane Todd-Crawford, was fully willing to be involved with what can only be described as experimental surgery in the face of uncertainty. She defi ed pain simply by reciting psalms and hymns, and was back at home within 4 weeks with no complications, ultimately living another 33 years.
