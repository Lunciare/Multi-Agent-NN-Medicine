Utilize the GP’s knowledge of the patient: they may have known them and/or their family for decades. He or she may even hold a ‘living will’ or advance directive if they cannot speak for themselves. Tactfully ask about alcohol, tobacco, and recreational drugs. How much? How long? When stopped? 1 unit = 8g of ethanol = 1 spirits measure = 1/2 glass of wine = 1/3 pint of beer. The CAGE questionnaire is a useful screening test for alcoholism (p281). Quantify smoking in terms of pack-years: 20 cigarettes/day for 1 year equals 1 pack-year. We all like to present ourselves well, so be inclined to double stated quantities (Holt’s ‘law’).
