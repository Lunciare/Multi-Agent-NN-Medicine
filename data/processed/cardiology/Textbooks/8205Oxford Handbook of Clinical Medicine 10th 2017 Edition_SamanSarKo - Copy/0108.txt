Frequently, the skills needed for diagnosis or treatment will lie beyond the team you are working for, so, during ward rounds, agree who should be asked for an opinion. You will be left with the job of making the arrangements, so check before your senior leaves exactly what their question is. Don’t be intimidated, but follow these simple rules: • Know the history and examination fi ndings (ideally your own), and have the pa- tient’s notes, observations, recent test results, and drug charts to hand (table 2.2). • At the outset, state if you are just looking for advice or if you are asking if the patient could be seen.
