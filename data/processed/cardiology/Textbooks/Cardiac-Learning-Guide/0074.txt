The categories progress from an NYHA class I, indicating the patient has no symptoms with normal physical activity, all the way to an NYHA class IV, indicating the patient has symptoms of heart failure at rest or with the slightest physical activity.58 The NYHA classification system can predict mortality in heart failure and can be a useful way to monitor the effectiveness of treatment.50,58 Another classification scheme from the American College of Cardiology Foundation (ACC) and the American Heart Association (AHA) defines heart failure according to symptoms and structural changes to the heart (Figure 4-2).
