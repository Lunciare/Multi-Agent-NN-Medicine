Pathophysiology The shunt of blood from the left atrium to the right atrium results in: increased volume load and dilatation of the right atrium and right ventricle (RV) increased pulmonary blood flow and enlargement of the pulmonary arteries increase in size of the pulmonary veins reduced filling of the left ventricle (LV) and aorta 2/6 Over time, the aorta and LV shrink, and pulmonary vascular resistance increases and causes Eisenmenger syndrome (see below).
