After the relevant surgical procedure, a full course of antibiotic eradication therapy should be administered. Prognosis 6/7 With effective treatment, patients with IE have a 70% survival rate. The prognosis is worse if there is no identifiable organism or if there is a resistant organism. Fungal infections are associated with increased mortality, as is prosthetic valve endocarditis. The most common cause of death is intractable heart failure. Prophylaxis All patients at risk for IE should receive antibiotic cover for invasive procedures (see Tables 2 and 3, overleaf).
