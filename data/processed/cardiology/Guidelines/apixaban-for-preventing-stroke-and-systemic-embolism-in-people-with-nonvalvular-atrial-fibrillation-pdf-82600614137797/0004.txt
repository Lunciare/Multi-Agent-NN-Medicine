ARISTOTLE included adults with atrial fibrillation or atrial flutter not resulting from a reversible cause and at least 1 additional risk factor for stroke (assessed by CHADS criteria). It enrolled 2 patients from 39 countries; 40% of participants were from Europe and this included patients from 41 sites in the UK. The average age was 69 years and 65% of the population were male. The mean time in therapeutic range for patients in the warfarin arm was 62.2%, and the median time in therapeutic range was 66%. Approximately 4% of the study population received 2.5 mg apixaban (those who had 2 or more of the following criteria: 80 years or older, a body weight of 60 kg or less, or a serum creatinine level of 1.5 mg/100 ml [133 micromole/l] or more).
