NICE guideline NG128 (2019, updated 2022), recommendation 1.3.1 • The 4-hour timeframe is based on the SSNAP target Definitions of terms used in this quality statement Admission to a specialist acute stroke unit Admission should be within 4 hours of arrival at the A&E department for adults with suspected stroke, following an initial assessment (unless their care needs should be provided elsewhere, such as an intensive care unit). [Adapted from NICE's guideline on stroke and transient ischaemic attack in over 16s, recommendation 1.3.1, and SSNAP] Specialist acute stroke unit A discrete area in the hospital designated for people with stroke. It is staffed by a specialist stroke multidisciplinary team, who have access to equipment for monitoring and rehabilitation.
