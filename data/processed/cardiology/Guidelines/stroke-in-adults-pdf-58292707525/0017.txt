This means that they are supported to go home from hospital as early as possible and have the same rehabilitation care at home. This is only offered if the person is well enough and it can be done safely. Source guidance Stroke rehabilitation in adults. NICE guideline NG236 (2023), recommendation 1.1.9 Definitions of terms used in this quality statement Early supported discharge An intervention for people who have had a stroke that allows care to be transferred from an inpatient environment to a community setting to continue rehabilitation. The intensity of care and the expertise of those providing it is maintained. [Adapted from NICE's guideline on stroke rehabilitation in adults] Suitable for early supported discharge The core multidisciplinary stroke team will assess whether early supported discharge is suitable for adults who have had a stroke. The assessment takes into account the person's functional, cognitive and social circumstances.
