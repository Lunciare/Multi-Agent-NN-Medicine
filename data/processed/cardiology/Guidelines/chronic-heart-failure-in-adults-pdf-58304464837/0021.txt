Chronic heart failure in adults
KEYWORDS: heart, failure, heart failure, chronic, chronic heart, adults, quality, data, statement, adults chronic, nice, specialist, care, cardiac, fraction

Audit contains data on hospital admission rates for heart failure. What the quality statement means for different audiences Service providers (GP practices, hospitals and community providers) ensure that systems are in place so that adults with chronic heart failure have a review within 2 weeks of any change in the dose or type of their heart failure medication. Healthcare professionals (such as GPs, specialists in cardiac care, heart failure specialist nurses, specialist multidisciplinary heart failure teams, and clinical pharmacists with an interest in chronic heart failure) carry out a review for adults with chronic heart failure within 2 weeks of any change in the dose or type of their heart failure medication. The multidisciplinary heart failure team will decide who is the most appropriate team member to do this, for example, the GP may lead the care in consultation with other members of the team. Commissioners (integrated care systems) ensure that they commission services in which adults with chronic heart failure have a review within 2 weeks of any change in the dose or type of their heart failure medication.
