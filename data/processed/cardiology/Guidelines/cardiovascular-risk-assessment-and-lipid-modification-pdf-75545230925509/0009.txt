Healthcare professionals (such as GPs, nurses, healthcare support workers, and pharmacists) give advice on diet and lifestyle changes for the primary prevention of CVD to adults with a 10-year risk of CVD of 10% or more within 3 months of their cardiovascular risk assessment score being recorded. They take the person's individual needs, preferences and circumstances into account when giving advice. Commissioners ensure that they commission services that can deliver diet and lifestyle advice to adults with a 10-year risk of CVD of 10% or more within 3 months of their cardiovascular risk assessment score being recorded. Adults with a 1 in 10 chance or more of developing CVD in the next 10 years (a 10-year risk of 10% or more) are given advice on diet and lifestyle changes, such as stopping smoking, losing weight, eating a healthy diet and exercising when they are found to be at risk.
