[2010] 1.3.3 Making a diagnosis based on clinical assessment 1.3.3.1 Assess the typicality of chest pain as follows: • Presence of three of the features below is defined as typical angina. • Presence of two of the three features below is defined as atypical angina. • Presence of one or none of the features below is defined as non-anginal chest pain. Anginal pain is: • constricting discomfort in the front of the chest, or in the neck, shoulders, jaw or arms • precipitated by physical exertion • relieved by rest or GTN within about 5 minutes. [2010, amended 2016] 1.3.3.2 Do not define typical and atypical features of anginal chest pain and non-anginal chest pain differently in men and women.
