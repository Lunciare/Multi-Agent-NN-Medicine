[2013] 1.1.11 Before and during early supported discharge: • provide the person after stroke, and their family members and carers, with information about early supported discharge, including details of who to contact if problems arise, to support shared decision making about their care • assign a member of the early supported discharge team or the stroke rehabilitation service (for example, a stroke key worker) to the person to coordinate their care • take into account the needs of family members and carers and offer relevant training and support to help reduce caregiver strain, in line with NICE's guideline on supporting adult carers • be aware, and ensure family members and carers understand, that the person's psychological needs can change after stroke (for recommendations on identifying and managing psychological problems, see the section on psychological functioning). [2023] 1.1.12 Before transfer from hospital to home or to a care setting, discuss and agree a health and social care plan with the person after stroke, and their family members and carers (as appropriate), and provide this to all relevant health and social care providers.
