The decision on whether repair is preferred over conservative management should be made jointly by the person and their clinician after assessment of a number of factors, including: • aneurysm size and morphology • the person's age, life expectancy, fitness for surgery, and any other conditions they have • the risk of AAA rupture if they do not have repair • the short- and long-term benefits and risks, and the other disadvantages of repair such as having to stay in hospital, the risks of the operation, the recovery period, the potential need for further procedures and the need for surveillance imaging appointments • the uncertainties around estimates of risk for AAAs larger than 5.5 cm (measured inner-to-inner maximum anterior-posterior aortic diameter on ultrasound).
