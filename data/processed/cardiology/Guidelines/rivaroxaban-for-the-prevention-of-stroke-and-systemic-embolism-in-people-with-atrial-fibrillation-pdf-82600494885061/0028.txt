The ERG's revised analysis for rivaroxaban compared with warfarin produced an incremental cost of £1,815, an incremental QALY of 0.061 and an ICER of £29,537 per QALY gained. 3.30 See the manufacturer's submission and the ERG report for full details of all the evidence. 4 Consideration of the evidence 4.1 The Appraisal Committee reviewed the data available on the clinical and cost effectiveness of rivaroxaban, having considered evidence on the nature of stroke and systemic embolism and the value placed on the benefits of rivaroxaban by people with atrial fibrillation, those who represent them, and clinical specialists. It also took into account the effective use of NHS resources. 4.2 The Committee was aware that the main concerns for people with atrial fibrillation were fear of having a stroke and anxiety about the difficulty of keeping the INR within the therapeutic range.
