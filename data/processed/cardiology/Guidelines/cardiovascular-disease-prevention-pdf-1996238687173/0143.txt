As CVD may not be seen as an immediate concern within targeted communities, staff may first need to listen and respond to the existing concerns of the community. This may be done through participating in existing networks and forums, or creating forums that have more open agendas, at least to start with. Sufficient time is needed to ensure that this is done appropriately and also to ensure that changes become adopted by the community so that they are empowered to continue, even if the project itself comes to an end. Information and education is likely to be more effective if it relates to the experiences of the community, and if those that deliver it are seen as part of that community. Appropriately skilled staff are needed for effective community engagement. Greater levels of participation, that involve community members as partners or devolve power to them, may have additional benefits, ensuring that programmes are truly responsive to community needs, involving local people in the complexities of planning and delivering such programmes and so facilitating understanding within the community.
