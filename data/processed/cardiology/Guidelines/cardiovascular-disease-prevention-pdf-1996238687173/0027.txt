Recommendation 14 Regional CVD prevention programmes – preparation What action should be taken? • Gain a good understanding of the prevalence and incidence of CVD in the community. Find out about any previous CVD prevention initiatives that have been run (including any positive or negative experiences). • Consider how existing policies relating to food, tobacco control and physical activity, including those developed by the local authority, may impact on the prevalence of CVD locally. • Gauge the community's level of knowledge of, and beliefs about, CVD risk factors.
