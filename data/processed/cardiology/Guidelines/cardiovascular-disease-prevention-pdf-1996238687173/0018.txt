Recommendation 8 Common agricultural policy • The common agricultural policy (CAP) is the overarching framework used by EU member countries to form their own agricultural policies. The burden of diet-related disease has grown considerably since CAP was first implemented. • CAP reform offers a significant opportunity to address the burden of CVD. However, there are still a number of significant 'distortions' in relation to certain food prices and production processes which potentially increase the burden of disease. Further reform should aim to remove these distortions to promote health and wellbeing and to provide a basis for UK government action to prevent CVD (Lloyd-Williams et al. 2008 and Lock et al. 2005). • The CAP has 2 main 'pillars': market measures (first pillar) and rural development policy (second pillar).
