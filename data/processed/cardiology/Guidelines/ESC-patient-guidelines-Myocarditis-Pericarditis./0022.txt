Don’t stop them on your own. Mention any side effects – there may be alternative medications. • Be your own advocate: Keep a symptoms diary if you feel it is needed. Note any new chest pain, palpitations or worsening breathlessness. Share these with your doctor/nurse. You can also track your own blood pressure and weight – sudden weight gain might signal fluid retention – but try not to focus only on your disease as getting on with your life is important. • Prepare questions for your healthcare visits: Use patient portals or nurse lines if you have concerns between visits. • Bring a partner or family member to appointments: They might remember details or ask good questions. But only use this option if you feel it is right for you. • Ask about referrals: If needed, your doctor may refer you to specialists (e.g. an electrophysiologist for arrhythmias, a rheumatologist for autoimmune causes or a cardiac rehabilitation programme). • Support: Inquire about resources like nutrition counselling or support groups.
