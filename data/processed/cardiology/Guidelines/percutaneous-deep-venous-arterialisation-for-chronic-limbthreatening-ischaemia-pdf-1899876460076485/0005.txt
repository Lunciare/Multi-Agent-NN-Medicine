Additional documentation in confidence provided by a company was also considered by the committee. 3.2 The professional experts and the committee considered the key efficacy outcomes to be: improvement in limb perfusion and wound healing, reduction in major amputation, and improvement in quality of life. 3.3 The professional experts and the committee considered the key safety outcomes to be: pain, bleeding, infection, worsening of perfusion, and steal syndrome. 3.4 Three commentaries from people who have had this procedure were discussed by the committee.
