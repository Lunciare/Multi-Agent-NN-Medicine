The committee agreed that, although the ICERs presented did not include the people at high risk who had a myocardial infarction more than 2 years ago and whose antiplatelet therapy had been stopped less than 1 year ago, the recommendation should cover this group. 4.11 The committee recognised that all the cost-effectiveness evidence assumed a maximum treatment length of 3 years. It understood that some clinicians and patients may want to continue treatment indefinitely, but that the costs and clinical benefits of doing so had not been presented. The committee therefore concluded that the positive recommendation should only be for the length of time for which evidence had been presented, specifically 3 years.
