Clinical diagnosis of FH Assessment for a clinical diagnosis of FH should use all 3 criteria below: • exclusion of secondary causes of hypercholesterolaemia • 2 measurements of LDL-C concentration • assessment against Simon Broome or Dutch Lipid Clinic Network criteria to make a clinical diagnosis of FH in primary care settings. [Adapted from NICE's guideline on familial hypercholesterolaemia, recommendations 1.1.4, 1.1.5 and 1.1.9] Equality and diversity considerations The statement has been restricted to adults because the criteria for assessment for a clinical diagnosis of FH are not appropriate for children and young people under 16 years. Quality statement 2: Specialist referral Quality statement People with a clinical diagnosis of familial hypercholesterolaemia (FH) are referred for specialist assessment. Rationale Diagnosing and managing FH in an individual and their relatives can be complex, and is best achieved when there is access to specialist services. Specialist assessments, which include DNA testing, can confirm a diagnosis.
