As long as the services are compliant with NHS communication networks such as the N3 network or health and social care network, no changes to current infrastructure are needed. No published reports on the resource consequences of adopting the technology were found. Broomwell Healthwatch provided the results of 3 audits of pilot studies performed in Lancashire and Cumbria, Greater Manchester and Greater Birmingham. Cost savings were estimated based on secondary care referrals that were prevented in 61 to 65% of the cases. In Greater Birmingham, 1,934 audit forms were returned and the estimated cost savings were £97,672. In Greater Manchester, 2,377 secondary care referrals were prevented out of 3,732 ECGs, leading to estimated cost savings of £358,927.
