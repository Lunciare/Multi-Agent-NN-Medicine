In addition, the use of antiplatelet drugs and other therapeutic strategies to prevent thrombosis have improved long-term outcomes. 2.11 One of the main criteria for assessing the clinical effectiveness of PTCA with stents compared with standard PTCA (without stents) is the ability to reduce the incidence of subsequent attacks of angina as well as major adverse coronary events (MACE), which include death, MI and the need for further revascularisation procedures (CABG or repeat PCI). 2.12 Patients for whom both a CABG and a PCI involving stenting are appropriate techniques would, other things being equal, choose PCI in almost all cases, even though the chances of restenosis are greater.
