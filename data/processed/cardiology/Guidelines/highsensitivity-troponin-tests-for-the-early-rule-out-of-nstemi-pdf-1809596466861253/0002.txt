Healthcare professionals should consider the likely time since the onset of symptoms when interpreting test results. 1.3 When NSTEMI is not ruled out using early rule-out test strategies, use NICE's guideline on recent-onset chest pain of suspected cardiac origin to help diagnose myocardial infarction, and consider using sex-specific thresholds at the 99th percentile (see section 4.7 and section 5.2). 1.4 Further research is recommended on the diagnostic performance of the TriageTrue High Sensitivity Troponin I test when using samples at point of care (see section 5.1). Why the committee made these recommendations When someone comes to a hospital emergency department with chest pain, tests are needed to work out if they're having a myocardial infarction (heart attack), and if so, what type it is and what treatment they need.
