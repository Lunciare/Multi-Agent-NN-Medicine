The committee agreed they could not make a recommendation on telemonitoring because the evidence was not sufficient to show a clear benefit and the studies were inconsistent in the telemonitoring methods used. How the recommendations might affect practice The recommendations reflect current practice, so there should be no change in practice. They will encourage appropriate and suitable training to be given so that both people with hypertension and their healthcare professionals are confident that blood pressure is being measured properly using home monitoring devices.
