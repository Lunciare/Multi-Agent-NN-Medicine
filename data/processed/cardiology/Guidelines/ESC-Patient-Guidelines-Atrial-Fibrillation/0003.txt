AF is a common condition, but the effect it has on individual patients can vary widely. Some will have very few symptoms, whilst in others, AF can be disabling. Common symptoms related to AF Dizzyness Disturbed Palpitations sleep Chest pain Worries Fatigue and Breathlessness less active AF can lead to several health problems, some of which are serious. For example, blood clots that develop in patients with AF may cause a stroke if they travel to the brain, or can impact on other body organs. In the longer term, patients can develop heart failure which causes breathlessness, as the heart cannot pump enough blood to cope with daily activity. AF is also associated with a higher risk of dying, being admitted to hospital for any reason and developing certain types of dementia.
