Stroke and transient ischaemic attack in over 16s: diagnosis and initial management

Overview This guideline covers interventions in the acute stage of a stroke or transient ischaemic attack (TIA). It offers the best clinical advice on the diagnosis and acute management of stroke and TIA in the 48 hours after onset of symptoms. Who is it for? • Healthcare professionals in primary and secondary NHS healthcare settings • Commissioners and providers of services • People aged over 16 who have had a stroke or TIA, their families and carers Recommendations People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in NICE's information on making decisions about your care. Making decisions using NICE guidelines explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.
