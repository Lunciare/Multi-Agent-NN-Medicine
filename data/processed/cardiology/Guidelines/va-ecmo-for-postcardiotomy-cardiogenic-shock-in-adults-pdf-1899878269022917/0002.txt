Healthcare professionals must also inform the clinical governance leads in their organisation if they want to do the procedure. Auditing of outcomes Healthcare professionals doing this procedure should collect data on safety and outcomes of the procedure. Enter details about everyone having this procedure into the Extracorporeal Life Support Organization registry and regularly review the data on outcomes and safety. Who should be involved with the procedure Patient selection should be done by a multidisciplinary team. The procedure could be done in centres specialising in managing PCS and by healthcare professionals with specific training in this procedure. What evidence generation is needed Healthcare professionals must collect data specifically around the safety and efficacy of this procedure, including on: • patient selection • the technology used • short- and long-term survival outcomes • complication rates.
