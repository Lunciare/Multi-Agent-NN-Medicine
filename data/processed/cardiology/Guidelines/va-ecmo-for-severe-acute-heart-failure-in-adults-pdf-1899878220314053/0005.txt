For people with poor kidney function, a haemofiltration unit may be added to the circuit. 3 Committee discussion The condition 3.1 Acute heart failure is a complex clinical syndrome of symptoms and signs that happen when the efficiency of the heart as a pump is impaired. It can lead to reduced blood flow to the body and increased filling pressures in the heart. Cardiogenic shock is the most severe form of acute heart failure, with short-term mortality between 30% and 50%. It can be caused by a heart attack, heart failure, inflammation of the heart muscle, drug overdoses and poisoning, and blood clots in the lungs. Severe acute heart failure in pregnancy is relatively uncommon but rates are increasing, particularly in the postpartum period.
