Regardless of treatment, new varicose veins develop, and treatment often must be repeated indefinitely. Key Points Varicose veins are more common in women than in men. Symptoms may include fullness, fatigue, pressure, bleeding and pain or hyperesthesia in the legs; stasis dermatitis and venous stasis ulcers are uncommon. Treatment may include compression stockings, sclerotherapy, minimally invasive surgery, or thermal ablation Regardless of treatment, varicose veins often recur.
