Clues to the presence of pre-excited atrial fibrillation include a very rapid, dominantly wide QRS complex arrhythmia with the occasional occurrence of a narrow QRS complex that is not the result of a long proceeding RR interval. The QRS complex morphology may vary, but all QRS complexes are a fusion of that of the widest QRS complex and that of a normal narrow QRS complex. In pre-excited atrial fibrillation, the normal rate-limiting effects of the AV node are bypassed, and the resultant excessive ventricular rates (sometimes 200 to 300 beats/minutes) may lead to ventricular fibrillation (2).
