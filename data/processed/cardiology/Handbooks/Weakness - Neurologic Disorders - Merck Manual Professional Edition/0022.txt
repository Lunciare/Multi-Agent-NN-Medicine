Weakness that fluctuates from day to day may be caused by multiple sclerosis and sometimes metabolic myopathies. Weakness that fluctuates over the course of a day may be caused by myasthenia gravis, Eaton-Lambert syndrome, or periodic paralysis. The anatomic pattern of weakness is characterized by difficulty performing specific motor tasks. Anatomic patterns suggest certain diagnoses: Proximal muscle weakness impairs reaching upward (eg, combing hair, lifting objects over the head), ascending stairs, or getting up from a sitting position; this pattern is typical of inflammatory myopathies. Distal muscle weakness impairs tasks such as stepping over a curb, holding a cup, writing, buttoning, or using a key; this pattern is typical of polyneuropathies and myotonic dystrophy.
