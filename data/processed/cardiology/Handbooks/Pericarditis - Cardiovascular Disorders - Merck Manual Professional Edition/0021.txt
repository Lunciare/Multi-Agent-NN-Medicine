Pearls & Pitfalls Significant cardiac tamponade is a clinical diagnosis; echocardiographic findings alone are not an indication for pericardiocentesis. If tamponade is suspected but not yet confirmed (eg, by clinical findings and echocardiography), right heart (Swan-Ganz) catheterization may be done.
