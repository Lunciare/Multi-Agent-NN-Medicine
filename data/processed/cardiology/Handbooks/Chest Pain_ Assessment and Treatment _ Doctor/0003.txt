Psychological - eg, anxiety, depression, panic disorder. Others: sickle cell crisis, diabetic mononeuritis, tabes dorsalis. Assessment The aim is to exclude a life-threatening cause, which needs immediate treatment, from other causes of chest pain. Diagnosis of chest pain is difficult but the history of- ten gives an indication of the underlying cause. As the patient walks into the consultation room or, when listening carefully over the telephone, discern general appearance/status, including any confusion, anxiety, shortness of breath, pain, distress, whether pale or sweaty, and any vomiting.
