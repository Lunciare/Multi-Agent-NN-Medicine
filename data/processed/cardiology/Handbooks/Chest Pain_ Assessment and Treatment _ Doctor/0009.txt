Management of chest pain Management will be dependent on diagnosis. See the separate articles on Stable angina, Acute coronary syndrome and Acute myocardial infarction. Psychological treatment may be helpful with some patients. Prognosis There is a 3% increased mortality rate in the year following consulting a GP re- garding the onset of chest pain compared with a control group with no chest pain, the excess being primarily due to cardiovascular disease. 7/9 Once a cardiac cause for chest pain is excluded, further investigation is often curtailed and many patients continue to experience undiagnosed chest pain, which carries significant psychological morbidity.
