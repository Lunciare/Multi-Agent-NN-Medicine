Disadvantages of MRI MRI is relatively expensive, requires longer imaging times than CT, and may not be immediately available in all areas. Other disadvantages include problems related to: The magnetic field Patient claustrophobia Contrast reactions Magnetic field MRI is relatively contraindicated in patients with implanted materials that can be affected by powerful magnetic fields. These materials include: Ferromagnetic metal (ie, containing iron) Magnetically activated or electronically controlled medical devices (eg, pacemakers, implantable cardioverter defibrillators, cochlear implants) Nonferromagnetic electrical wires or materials (eg, some pacemaker wires, certain pulmonary artery catheters) Ferromagnetic material may be displaced by the strong magnetic field, injuring a nearby organ; for example, displacement of vascular clips can result in hemorrhage.
