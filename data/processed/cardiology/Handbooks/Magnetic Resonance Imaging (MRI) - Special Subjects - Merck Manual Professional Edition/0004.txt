MRI can be substituted for CT with contrast in patients with a high risk of reactions to iodinated contrast agents. Contrast agents Contrast agents are often used with MRI to highlight vascular structures and to help characterize inflammation and tumors. The most commonly used agents are gadolinium derivatives, which have magnetic properties that affect proton relaxation times. MRI of intra-articular structures may include injection of a diluted gadolinium derivative into a joint.
