AHA in 2010 was performing CPR by observer or by the patient with least or even no facilities.[10] CPR is a practical proficiency and needs professional guide followed up by ordered practice on a resuscitation mannequin to gain and maintain full competency. In most CPR classes, a simple mnemonic is used to aid memory of the clinical approach to the unconscious patient and CPR. The most common one used worldwide was ABC which changed to CAB based on AHA guideline since 2010 which stands for Circulation, Airway and Breathing.[10] CPR skills are not confined to medical professionals, but are regularly taught to members of the public. Widespread knowledge of CPR has a community benefit, as CPR must be applied quickly after a patients heart has stopped. Early CPR in the community is essential to the prevention of brain damage during a cardiac arrest and increases the chance of survival.
