Raynaud Phenomenon

may be temporary. Key Points Raynaud phenomenon is reversible vasospasm of parts of the hand in response to cold or emotional stress. Raynaud phenomenon may be primary or secondary to another disorder, typically a systemic rheumatic disease (eg, systemic sclerosis). Primary Raynaud phenomenon, unlike the secondary form, rarely causes gangrene or tissue loss. Diagnose clinically but consider testing to diagnose a suspected cause. Avoid cold, smoking, and any other triggers. Give a vasodilating calcium channel blocker or other medications.
