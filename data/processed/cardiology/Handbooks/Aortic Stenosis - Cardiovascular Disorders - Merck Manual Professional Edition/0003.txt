In medically underserved countries, the most common cause of aortic stenosis in all age groups is: Rheumatic fever Supravalvular AS caused by a discrete congenital membrane or narrowing of the sinotubular junction or ascending aorta is uncommon. Supravalvular AS can be a component of Williams syndrome, a microdeletion syndrome of chromosome 7 associated with a characteristic facial appearance (high and broad forehead, hypertelorism, strabismus, upturned nose, long philtrum, wide mouth, dental abnormalities, puffy cheeks, micrognathia, low-set ears) and neonatal hypercalcemia. Subvalvular AS caused by a congenital membrane or fibrous ring just beneath the aortic valve is uncommon.
