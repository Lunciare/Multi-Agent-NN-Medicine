Key Points Acute limb ischemia is characterized by severe pain, pallor, paresthesias (or anesthesia), poikilothermia (cold sensation), and pulselessness in the affected extremity. Treatment consists of embolectomy, thrombolysis, or bypass surgery. Despite treatment, approximately 11% of patients with acute limb ischemia require limb amputation.
