Med Sci Monit. 2022;28:e935135. doi:10.12659/MSM.935135 Key Points Only about 50% of patients in the United States with hypertension receive treatment, and about one-quarter of those patients have adequate blood pressure (BP) control. Most hypertension is primary; only 5 to 15% is secondary to another disorder (eg, primary aldosteronism, renal parenchymal disease). Severe or prolonged hypertension damages the cardiovascular system, brain, and kidneys, increasing risk of myocardial infarction, stroke, and chronic kidney disease. Hypertension is usually asymptomatic until complications develop in target organs. When hypertension is newly diagnosed, do a urinalysis, spot urine albumin:creatinine ratio, blood tests (creatinine, potassium, sodium, calcium, fasting plasma glucose, lipid panel, and thyroid-stimulating hormone), and ECG. Determine the 10-year cardiovascular risk, using the PREVENT calculator. Reduce BP to < 130/80 mm Hg for most patients..
