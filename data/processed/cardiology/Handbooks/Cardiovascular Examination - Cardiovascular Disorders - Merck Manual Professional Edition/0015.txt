The apical impulse in healthy individuals should be palpable between the fourth and fifth intercostal space just medial to the midclavicular line and cover an area < 2 to 3 cm in diameter. A central precordial heave, also called a parasternal heave or lift, is a palpable lifting sensation under the sternum and anterior chest wall to the left of the sternum; it suggests significant right ventricular hypertrophy or dilation.
